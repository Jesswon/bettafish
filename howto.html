How to Setup Tank

Before starting your new betta aquarium it's important to find out what you need to ensure that your betta has a long & healthy life. Here is a guide to all the equipments you need to properly setup a tank and the setup process.

  • Tank
  • Filter
  • Heater
  • Substrate
  • Gravel Cleaner
  • Water Test Kit
  • Water Conditioner
  • Fish Net
  • Decorations
  • Setup Process

Tank

A 5-gallon tank is sufficient for a betta because it provides them a lot of space. Avoid tank size less than 5 gallon. 1 gallon is too small, and your betta will not live to its maximum lifespan. Also avoid bowls. Co2 can't do proper oxygen exchange in bowls because it's a circle and lacks open space.

Filter

It's essential to provide a low-flow filter. Filters with strong flow will exhaust your fish because it will need to keep swimming against the current. Buy a filter with adjustable flow or buy a pre-filter sponge and wrap it around the filter nozzle to reduce the flow. Filter cartridges should be rinsed in aquarium water (to clean).

Heater

A heater is a must! Although bettas can survive without one, it's important to keep them warm and prevent them from getting sick. Set the temperature to 78-82°. Most heaters come with thermometer stickers to help you monitor the temperature.

Substrate

Choose either natural pebbles or sand. Make sure to rinse the new aquarium gravel couple times before placing them inside the aquarium.

Gravel Cleaner

Purchase a gravel cleaner to use during water changes.

Water Test Kit

If you want to check the water condition, especially during tank cycling, then you can purchase these kits to visually check the levels of pH, nitrite, nitrate, and ammonia. A recommended kit is API Freshwater Master Test Kit.

Water Conditioner

A water conditioner is necessary to get rid of harmful contaminants like chlorine, chloramines, ammonia. Use this during every water change; there are a lot of great brands to select from.

Fish Net

A fish net is used to scoop the betta in and out of its tank, especially during a water change. Make sure the mesh is soft.

Decorations

Decors & accessories help add color to your aquarium and, most importantly, keep your bettas happy.

Plants

Plants come in varieties. You can choose plastic or live plants. Make sure that artificial plants don't have sharp edges that could rip your betta's fins and tail. If you're opting for plastic plants, then it's best to buy silk and non-toxic ones.

Ornaments

Examples of ornaments are cave hideouts, driftwood, leaf pad, tree stump holes, and many more. Betta's need a comfortable place to hide and rest. Caves, ornaments with holes, and leaf pads are really the best options. Ensure that the decoration pieces don't have any sharp edges and paint chips.

Miscellaneous

Other optional things to put include Indian Almond Leaves and an air pump (bubbler). Indian Almond Leaves or Catappa are large brown or dark orange leaves that naturally enhances the fish's skin (prevent fin rot), cures bacterial diseases, and prevents the fish from getting stressed. Just place one inside. It will decompose, turning the water into a murky, brownish color. Additionally, an air pump can be added to to your tank to provide more oxygen.

Setup Process

  1. Rinse your substrate and decors in warm water.

  2. Place your substrate and decors in your tank and then fill with water.

  3. Plug in and turn on the filter.

  4. Add water conditioner. Product will specify how much to put in.

  5. Cycle your new aquarium, testing for ammonia, nitrite, nitrate, pH levels.

  6. Do a 50% water change once ammonia and nitrate are below 0.2ppm.

  7. Put betta in its new home.
