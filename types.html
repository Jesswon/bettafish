Types

Bettas can be differentiated by the shape of their tails. Here is a list of tails: crowntail, veiltail, plakat, halfmoon, double tail, elephant ear, delta tail, rosetail, comb tail, round tail, spadetail, over-halfmoon, super delta, half sun, feather tail. Bettas also come in a wide variaty of colors and patterns.

Common Betta Fishes

Veiltail Betta


Veiltail is the most common and widely available tail type for betta fish. For males, the tail is long, flowing and downwards swooping. Females are lihther in color and have shorter fins compared to males. Its name comes from the fact that the tail looks like a veil as the fish swims. If you want to breed Betta fish, you should know this is a dominant tail type over other variations, and therefore is one of the easiest to breed.

Halfmoon Betta


Halfmoon tail creates half a circle, which resembles the shape of a half-moon. Its tail is large in size. Anal and dorsal fins are wider than other betta fish varieties. One thing to note about this tail type is it's strictly bred in captivity by experts to exhibit during Betta shows.

Crowntail Betta


Crowntail bettas, the most popular freshwater fish, are named for their long spikey and crown-like appearance of their tails. If the webbings reach more than 2/3 above the tail, then the fish is considered a Combtail. Males have dramatic colors with longer fins and tails, while females have lighter colors with shorter tail.

Plakat Betta


Plakat bettas are smaller compared to most betta fish. It has shorter and rounder tail, fin with a little body. In addition, their fins are also quite short when compared to other variations. Plakat bettas looks a lot like wild bettas; the name plakat derives from the Thai word “plakad” meaning fighting fish. Plakat bettas were commonly used and bred for fighting.

Elephant Ear Betta


Elephant Ear bettas are also called Dumbo Ear bettas because it has two huge pectoral fins that resemble elephant ears. It can be agressive and its large fins can intimidate other fishes. Elephant Ear are found in the wild exhibiting more dull coloration from dark green, grey, and brown. Among the aquarium trade, more striking colors are found such as bright blue, turquoise, red and yellow.

Delta Tail Betta


A Delta Tail Betta has a large tail that's shaped like a triangle. In fact, it is named after the Greek letter d which is a triangular-shaped symbol for delta. Its tail starts to get smaller as it approaches the body, and the edges become wider. Another variation is the Super Delta.

Rosetail Betta


Rosetail Betta, a variation of the half-moon, have tail rays that are branchy. Its caudal fin or tail looks like a rose due this branchiness. One downside to this tail type is that it is heavy, which makes it harder for the fish to swim.

Round Tail Betta


Roundtails are similar to Deltas and Spadetails. However, the shape of the tail sets them apart. Roundtails have completely rounded tail edges.

Spadetail Betta


As the name implies, the caudal fins of a Spadetail betta are shaped like a spade.

Over-Halfmoon Betta


Over-Halfmoon is a variation of the Halfmoon with similar fins. However, it's like an extreme version of it; the main difference is that the tail expands larger than half a circle.

Male vs. Female

Male and female bettas can be differentiated through observable traits, such as appearance and behavior.

Appearance

Male Betta fish are what you usually think of when the word “betta” comes up. Wild-caught Betta males are slightly larger and brighter than females. However, they have been bred in Thailand for centuries for long, flowing fins and punchy red and blue tones far stronger than wild males. Betta male colors are also strongly diet dependent. A diet rich in carotenoids, found in foods like shrimp and bloodworms, helps bring out the red and gold tones. Many prepared Betta formulas like Omega One Betta Food also contain higher levels of both protein (including whole fish over simply fish meal) and carotenoid boosts for better color. Compared to female Betta fish, males are noticeably slimmer and slower to move thanks to those long fins. Considering how aggressive males can be in courting the slight handicap is an aid to uninterested females. So long as a frustrated male Siamese Fighting fish is too slow to catch females, he can’t bully them too much.

Female Betta fish are almost always slightly more subdued in tone and lack the trailing fins of males. However, some modern color varieties produce female Betta fish with color patterns equally as brilliant as their partners. Female Bettas are noticeably stouter as well compared to males. While it’s especially visible when she’s full of eggs females are thicker all around and not as long as full-grown males.

Behavior

Male Betta fish are famously intolerant of one another. Since they have been selectively bred for not only color but aggression, they will flare gills, perform fin displays, and attempt to bite even their own reflection when visible. Unlike most tropical fish, Male Betta fish don’t always back down due to exhaustion or surrender. He will continue giving chase until he can’t see the other male. Even if one male wins he may eventually die due to stress or infection.

Male Betta fish are also bubble nest builders. If you’ve ever owned a Betta, then you’ve probably come across a small raft of bubbles along the glass edge or corner of an aquarium. In the wild male Bettas find floating plants and debris to anchor their bubble nests to. Once completed, he then seeks out and coaxes females back to his love raft. Their territorial fights keep other males away from spots with the thickest aquatic weeds and gentlest current.

Female Betta fish have inherited the same aggressive genes passed down the line but don’t need to express them like males do. In fact, because female Betta fish tend to be more amiable and social, they can be kept together in betta sororities!


Colors

Some Betta Fish might have a uniform color whereas others will be a mix of lots of different colors; they can be most colors of the rainbow, some will be black, white, electric blue, translucent skin, copper, green and more. Common colors are solid blue and red. Red is the most dominant color, while purple and green are the rarest colors. Other colors are black, pink, rose petal, yellow, white, mustard gas, and a number of different shades.

Patterns

Patterns of Bettas have been created through selective breeding over the years. Patterns include but not limited to dragon scale, butterfly, marble, koi. Dragon Scale Bettas are known for its metallic coloration; its body scales appear like those of a lizard or dragon. Butterfly Bettas have a distinct color pattern where one solid color spreads from the body to the tail halfway. The other half is a paler or iridescent color like white. Marble Bettas have a marble-like pattern with colors that look blotchy and irregular. Koi Bettas, as their pattern name suggests, resemble the Koi pond fish.
